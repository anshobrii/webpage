Discover Bihar

B - Buddha

Bihar is the land of Buddha, who attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree in Bodh Gaya. The state is home to many significant Buddhist sites, including Mahabodhi Temple, Nalanda University, and Rajgir Hot Springs.

I - Indus Valley Civilization

Bihar is one of the oldest inhabited places in the world and was once home to the ancient Indus Valley Civilization. The state is also associated with the Mahabharata and Ramayana epics, which depict the region's rich cultural heritage.

H - Handicrafts

Bihar is known for its exquisite handicrafts, including Madhubani paintings, Sujni embroidery, and Tikuli art. These crafts are intricately woven into the state's social and cultural fabric and reflect the people's creativity and artistic sensibilities.

A - Agriculture

Bihar is primarily an agrarian state and produces a variety of crops, including rice, wheat, and maize. The state's fertile land, abundant water resources, and favorable climate make it an ideal place for agriculture, and it is one of the largest producers of vegetables and fruits in India.

R - Rich History

Bihar has a rich history and has been ruled by many powerful dynasties such as the Maurya, Gupta, and Pala. The state is home to many ancient monuments, including the ruins of Nalanda University, which was once a center of learning in the world. Bihar is also associated with the great emperor Ashoka, who ruled over the region and made it a hub of Buddhism.
