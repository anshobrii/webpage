The cultural landscape of Bihar

Madhubani paintings

Madhubani painting is a traditional art form that originated in the Mithila region of Bihar. These paintings are known for their intricate designs and vibrant colors and are created using natural dyes and pigments.


Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is one of the most important festivals in Bihar and is dedicated to the worship of the Sun God. It is celebrated six days after Diwali and involves several rituals and offerings made to the Sun God.


Sama-Chakeva

Sama-Chakeva is a unique festival celebrated in the Mithila region of Bihar. This festival has both religious and cultural importance. it is celebrated in November and is dedicated to the affectionate bond of siblings. The celebration also marks the arrival of colorful migratory birds from the Himalayas.


Rajgir Mahotsava

Rajgir Mahotsava is a festival with a key focus on showcasing the dance and music of Bihar. This cultural event is held in Rajgir and organized annually since 1986. It is a three-day event, celebrated to highlight the historically and culturally rich heritage of Nalanda and Rajgir. Many renowned artists from all over the country take part in this cultural event.


Sonepur Mela

Sonepur Mela is a popular annual fair held in Sonepur, a town in Bihar. The fair is known for its diverse range of products, including livestock, handicrafts, and clothing. The fair also attracts pilgrims who come to take a dip in the holy river Gandak and to offer prayers at the Harihar Nath temple.


Darbhanga International Film Festival

Darbhanga International Film Festival is the international film festival of Bihar held every year in Darbhanga since 2013. Darbhanga IFF is celebrated for a better development of Cinema, Film, Art, and Culture in Bihar. Films, filmmakers, and artists from all over the world participate in the festival every year.


Chhau Dance

The simple tribal people of Bihar express their creative joy through the Chhau dance, which was originally a war dance, preformed in order to perfect fighting techniques. It has, over the years, evolved into a narrative ballet.



Jat-Jatin Dance

Jat-Jatin Dance of the Mithila region is performed by the females (Harijans?) where one person performs the role of Jat (the husband) and Jatin (the wife) wearing masks and goes through the story of their life. Specialy played on the night when the groom has gone for marriage with most of the males of the village.


Bamboo and Mud Houses

In rural Bihar, traditional homes are often made with bamboo and mud. These houses are designed to be sturdy and weather-resistant, and they are built to keep the interiors cool in the hot summer months. They are also environmentally friendly and sustainable.


Bungalow Houses

In urban areas of Bihar, bungalow-style houses are common. These houses are characterized by their spacious layouts, large gardens, and verandas. They often have a colonial-style architecture with high ceilings, large windows, and ornate detailing.


Pind Daan

Pind Daan is a ritual that is performed by Hindus to honor their ancestors. In Bihar, the ritual is performed at Gaya, a city known for its ancient Hindu temples. During the ritual, offerings are made to ancestors by performing various religious rites and rituals. It is believed that performing Pind Daan at Gaya brings peace to the souls of the departed ancestors.
